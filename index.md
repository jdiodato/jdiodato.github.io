**Hello! My name is Joe Diodato.**

I am a recent graduate of [Indiana University](https://www.indiana.edu/){:target="_blank"}, where earned my M.S.Ed. in [higher education and student affairs](https://education.indiana.edu/programs/higher-education-and-student-affairs.html). I am on the job market, and am interested in a variety of entry-level higher education and student affairs positions in New York City.

**Please click [here](https://github.com/jdiodato/jdiodato.github.io/raw/master/files/diodato-resume.pdf)to download a copy of my resume in PDF form.**

My professional interests include:
- Assessing curricular approaches to student learning.
- The use of computational tools in higher education and student affairs work. 
- The philosophical underpinnings of student affairs practice. 

I previously served [Residential Programs and Services](https://www.rps.indiana.edu/index.html)(RPS) as the Graduate Assistant for Assessment and Special Projects. In this position, I provided vision and leadership to support the development of a robust assessment program for the department's [residential curriculum](https://www.rps.indiana.edu/experience/curriculum/index.html). Other prior roles that I have held include include residence hall management and [student conduct](https://studentaffairs.indiana.edu/student-conduct/index.html). Before coming to IU, I earned my Bachelor of Arts in [classical studies](https://classics.uncg.edu/) from [The University of North Carolina at Greensboro](http://uncg.edu/).

In my free time I enjoy learning to play the Great Highland bagpipe, which I blog about [here](https://josephdiodatopiping.wordpress.com/). I also enjoy reading, computer programming, and can often be found in various neighborhood coffee shops.

This webpage documents my development as a budding scholar-practitioner, including my resume, artifacts of my work, and other links that may be of interest. I welcome you to explore further, and thanks for stopping by my little corner of the internet.
