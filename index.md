I am a master's student at [Indiana University](https://www.indiana.edu/){:target="_blank"}, where I study [higher education and student affairs](https://education.indiana.edu/programs/higher-education-and-student-affairs.html){:target="_blank"}. I am on the job market, and I hope to obtain a position that will allow me to utilize the various quantitative and computational skills that I have honed over the last few years. I am particularly interested in positions that have to do with program assessment and evaluation.

**Please click [here](https://github.com/jdiodato/jdiodato.github.io/raw/master/files/diodato-resume.pdf){:target="_blank"} to download a copy of my resume in PDF form.**

My professional interests include:
- Assessing curricular approaches to student learning 
- Quantitative methods and computational tools applied to higher education and student affairs 
- The statistical competency of student affairs practitioners.

I previously served [Residential Programs and Services](https://www.rps.indiana.edu/index.html){:target="_blank"} (RPS) as the Graduate Assistant for Assessment and Special Projects. In this position, I provided vision and leadership to support the development of a robust assessment program for the department's [residential curriculum](https://www.rps.indiana.edu/experience/curriculum/index.html){:target="_blank"}. Other prior roles that I have held include include residence hall management and [student conduct](https://studentaffairs.indiana.edu/student-conduct/index.html){:target="_blank"}. Before coming to IU, I earned my Bachelor of Arts in [classical studies](https://classics.uncg.edu/){:target="_blank"} from [The University of North Carolina at Greensboro](http://uncg.edu/){:target="_blank"}.

In my free time I enjoy learning to play the Great Highland bagpipe, which I blog about [here](https://josephdiodatopiping.wordpress.com/){:target="_blank"}. I also enjoy reading, computer programming, and can often be found in various neighborhood coffee shops.

This webpage documents my development as a budding scholar-practitioner, including my resume, artifacts of my work, and other links that may be of interest. I welcome you to explore further, and thanks for stopping by my little corner of the internet.
