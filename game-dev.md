In late January 2020, I had the idea to develop a learning community of student affairs gamers (#SAgamers) who have an interest in learning more about game development. The purpose of this webpage is to document the progress of this project and to keep any needed resources in a central location.

My vision is for our learning community to be **creative**, **organic**, and **curious**. Aside from tending to some of the backend matters (creating the group chat, curating resources, etc.I hope that this group really is driven by what would best meet the needs of group members.

# Group Updates

**1/26/2020**: Created a [Slack](https://slack.com/) group chat, which was surpringly easy to set up. The free tier should be more than adequate for our purposes. I tentatively think that we'll be using the (freely available) [C# Yellow Book](http://www.csharpcourse.com/?fbclid=IwAR2FZ3VKL16oFfgfZM6YsAYO0_e1im4b0d7R3Sd18xrZM1wA-krGkfarrKQ) by Rob Miles for programming basics. We'll follow that up with some of the [tutorials](https://learn.unity.com/) that are available from [Unity](https://unity.com/solutions/game). Please contact me directly via Twitter or e-mail (see below) for the Slack invite code.
