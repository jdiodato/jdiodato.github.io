# The Open HESA Project

## Some Background 

Early on in graduate school (circa 2017-2018), I developed a fascination with the idea of a free and open world of learning. Platforms such as [Khan Academy](https://www.khanacademy.org/) showed me how powerful open educational resources could be, and this lead me down a rabbithole of discovering some amazing OER's that were freely available. Having grown up with an aptitude and interest in technology, I remember some pretty powerful moments of self-directed learning coming from my study of computer programmers on YouTube such as [thenewboston](https://www.youtube.com/user/thenewboston) and [Derek Banas](https://www.youtube.com/user/derekbanas). I would inevitably devote most of my free time to music, an endeavor that continues to this day. While I spent most of my formative teenage years in the band room and traveling to band competitions, this spark for open resources always lingered in the back of my end.

Skipping ahead a few years, it is the spring 2020 semester -- my last semester of graduate studies at Indiana University Bloomington. I'm taking [EDUC-R678 Emerging Learning Technologies](http://curtbonk.com/Micro-Monster_Syllabus_R678_Spring_of_2020.htm) as an elective. The class is taught by [Dr. Curt Bonk](http://curtbonk.com/), a faculty member in the IU School of Education with a larger than life passion for open learning. The class offers a variety of options to satisfy course requirements, and so being the inquisitive person that I am, I pitched an idea to Prof. Bonk to implement an educational YouTube community in the same pencasting style made famous by Sal Khan at the Khan Academy. Prof. Bonk was very enthusiastic about my project, and I look forward to implementing it over the course of the semester. While this idea is currently satisfying a course requirement for R678, I hope that incorporating thie project into the class will give me a theoretical and practical foundation with which to jumpstart the project.
